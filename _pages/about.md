I am a PhD student in Mathematics and Statistics at The University of Melbourne, supervised by [Peter Taylor](https://findanexpert.unimelb.edu.au/profile/6214-peter-taylor). My research sits at the intersection of applied probability, mathematical statistics, and wireless communications. I am particularly interested in parameter estimation and mathematical models for wireless networks and localisation systems, and in using those models to understand how randomness in a physical environment affects system performance.

My PhD research is informed by collaborations with [Aihua Xia](https://findanexpert.unimelb.edu.au/profile/15452-aihua-xia), [Paul Keeler](https://hpaulkeeler.com/), and [Tingjin Chu](https://findanexpert.unimelb.edu.au/profile/795463-tingjin-chu). I am broadly interested in point processes, stochastic calculus, and other probability tools for analysing engineering systems.

Before beginning my PhD, I completed both my Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Mathematics and Statistics at The University of Melbourne under the supervision of Aihua Xia. My Master's thesis studied the performance of wireless localisation with random sensor networks. This work led to my current [arXiv preprint](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.06396), *Performance Analysis for Wireless Localization with Random Sensor Network*, which is under review.

I have also undertaken research projects on wireless-network modelling and compound Poisson approximation for clustered rare events. These projects are documented in my [blog posts](/year-archive/).
